# Middle School Science Lab Report Rubric

**Description:** A rubric for assessing the quality of science lab reports for middle school students, focusing on hypothesis, procedure, data analysis, and conclusions.

**Subject:** Science
**Grade Level:** Middle School
**Scoring Type:** points
**Maximum Score:** 100

---

## Criteria and Performance Levels

| Criterion | Exemplary (4) | Proficient (3) | Developing (2) | Beginning (1) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Hypothesis** (25 pts) | Hypothesis is clear, specific, and testable. | Hypothesis is clear and generally testable. | Hypothesis is vague and somewhat testable. | Hypothesis is unclear or not testable. |
| **Procedure** (25 pts) | Procedure is detailed, clear, and replicable. | Procedure is clear but lacks some detail. | Procedure is unclear or missing key steps. | Procedure is poorly described or not included. |
| **Data Analysis** (25 pts) | Analysis is thorough, insightful, and well-supported by data. | Analysis is complete and generally supported by data. | Analysis is incomplete or lacks sufficient support from data. | Analysis is missing or poorly constructed. |
| **Conclusions** (25 pts) | Conclusions are clear, logical, and directly related to findings. | Conclusions are clear and mostly logical but may lack depth. | Conclusions are unclear or not strongly supported by findings. | Conclusions are missing or do not relate to findings. |

### Criterion Descriptions

**Hypothesis**: Clarity and testability of the hypothesis.

**Procedure**: Detail and clarity of the procedure followed.

**Data Analysis**: Analysis and interpretation of data collected during the experiment.

**Conclusions**: Quality and clarity of conclusions drawn from the experiment.
